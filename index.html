About this monitor

This is a public monitor of how fast post-quantum cryptography (PQC) is reaching real websites. The main view focuses on Canada; the map compares Canada to other countries. Each site is contacted with a TLS handshake; we record its TLS version, the key-exchange group it negotiates (flagging the post-quantum hybrid X25519MLKEM768), its certificate signature, and the network / CDN that serves it.

Why it matters: an attacker can record encrypted traffic today and decrypt it once quantum computers arrive ("harvest now, decrypt later"). Post-quantum key exchange closes that window. Post-quantum certificate signatures are a separate, slower migration. No public certificate authority issues them yet.

The dataset is re-scanned periodically; use the Scan date menu to compare snapshots over time. Built for the NSERC Summer 2026 research project (E. Deveci, Dalhousie University).
